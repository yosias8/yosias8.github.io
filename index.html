  • Crystals

    Crystals are solid minerals with a well-organized molecular structure where all the atoms or ions are arranged in a regular grid pattern. Crystals can be formed when gas or pressurized molten rocks quickly cool down but most crystals grow from mineral rich water that slowly cools down or remains at the ideal temperature. However, Crystals always need a starting point which is a nucleus, also sometimes called a seed crystal which is formed in a process called crystal nucleation that occurs in the liquid or solution phase.

    Crystals can be found in nature, such as snowflakes, quartz, and salt, or they can be artificially grown in a laboratory. They have a variety of uses, from jewelry and decorative objects to electronics and medical devices. They are also valued for their beauty, and many people collect crystals for their aesthetic and spiritual properties.

  • The Cave

    1. Boiling Water

    Before the Cave was discovered, it was filled with groundwater for the last tens of thousands of years. This water was driven upward by a magma chamber located deeper in the earth until it found an opening and settled in the cave. This intrusive water contained the mineral anhydrite.

    2. Anhydrite

    Anhydrate remains stable at temperatures of 58℃ or higher but at lower temperatures, it begins to dissolve and regroups as gypsum. The magma underneath the chamber kept the cave at a warm temperature, however, the air-temperature was slightly below 58℃ which broke down the anhydrite and filled the water with calcium and sulfate.

    3. Gypsum

    These particles then began to regroup as a variety of gypsum called selenite, named after the ancient Greek goddess of the moon, Selene, appreciated for its beauty. Because the cave remained underwater, white selenite crystal were allowed to grow abundantly. A 2011 study argued that under the conditions, it would have taken anywhere from 500,000 to 900,000 years to form a single selenite crystal that's 1 meter in diameter. With many of the giant crystals being 4-6 meter long and some even reaching up to 4 meters in width and 12 meters in length, it’s possible this process spanned out over millions of years.

  • The Cave's History & Current state

    The cave, 120 meter deep in the Sierra de Naica Mountain, was discovered in the year 2000, when the Peñoles Mining company found the cave and pumped out the water before anyone realized it was full of giant crystals.

    However, it was evident very soon that the cave was not a hospitable place. The air-temperature was sitting at 47.1℃ and humidity levels were almost 100%, breathing in the air for longer than 10 minutes could risk fluids condensing inside a person's lungs which can be fatal.

    In 2017, the Peñoles company stopped the pumping and allowed groundwater to fill up the ground again. However, the cave had been exposed to air for nearly two decades and the structural integrity had likely been compromised. But with water back in the cave, the big hope is that the giant crystals continue to grow.
